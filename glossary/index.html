üë∑Sharpe RatioA metric to understand the performance (rate of return) associated to risk of a portfolio or asset. It assumes that there is a risk free rate of return available, such as the yield for a US Treasury bond. It also assumes that volatility of rate of return (represented as a standard deviation) is equal to risk. It is calculated by subtracting the risk free rate of return from the total rate of return and dividing by the standard deviation of the expected rate of return. A lower Sharpe ratio indicates performance due to higher risk and a higher Sharpe ratio indicates performance due to lower risk. When comparing portfolios and assets, those with a higher Sharpe ratio are considered favorable as their returns come from good investment rather than high risk.
